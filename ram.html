Rocket Arm Man

A polished indiegame built on the Unity Game Engine using C#

Description

Rocket Arm Man is a 2D physics-based platformer where your only means of transportation is the knockback from the explosions of your rockets, also known in the gaming community as "rocket jumping". The catch is, your rockets are your arms, and your arms don't grow back. Use your strange super powers to traverse 20 levels of increasingly difficult obstacle courses while you spiral into insanity from their rage-inducing nature.

Reusable Assets

Rocket Arm Man was developed with the future in mind, and created different reusable components ("prefabs" in Unity), such as red lasers of death which can be easily dropped into any level.

Interactive GUI

With my video editing and animation background, I knew that even the smallest details contribute to an overall professional and cohesive UI and UX. What you see here is the cursor in-game following a script to animate its rotation based on the velocity of the mouse.

Inclusive Design

Subtitles, video tutorials, subtitles, graphics settings, and supported for Mac and Windows, Rocket Arm Man was designed with accessibility in mind.

What I Learned

As my first major coding project taking place over the course of a year, I became familiar with the software development life cycle after developing and releasing countless software updates. This project introduced me specifically to C#, a language nearly identical to Java, which helped me learn most other languages in the future. I also extensively used concepts of polymorphism to organize code and create a world of assets that often borrow behavior from each other, which was necessary since the project involved a total of 41 separate C# scripts, with a total of 3203 lines of code.

Besides programming, I utlized Photoshop, Premiere Pro, and Audacity to create game assets like sprites, textures, videos, and sound effects. I also learned project management skills as I was organizing my progress on Trello, as well as documenting progress and bugs in a private Discord channel, teaching me how to communicate in project development. I also learned UI/UX skills while designing an easy to use GUI, a seamless level design, and menus.
