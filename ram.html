Rocket Arm Man

A polished indiegame built on the Unity Game Engine using C#

How I built it

The 2D platformer game is a testament to self-motivation and the power of self-education in modern game development. Over the course of a year, I immersed myself in learning C# and the Unity game engine, meticulously writing 41 C# scripts that spanned over 3200 lines of code, demonstrating the complexity and feature-rich nature of the game. My commitment extended to the creation of visual elements, where I harnessed the capabilities of Photoshop to craft all of the game's graphical assets. With an eye for detail and gameplay mechanics, I designed 20 challenging levels that offer engaging experiences to players. Understanding the importance of presentation, I produced six professional video tutorials using Premiere Pro, showcasing the gameplay and features which were essential for marketing the game. The culmination of this project saw the game's successful publication on itch.io, a popular platform for independent game developers. Marketing efforts were vigorous and hands-on; I personally reached out to 200 Youtubers and Twitch streamers, leveraging social media platforms to generate buzz and attract players. This initiative not only spread the word about the game but also underscored the importance of community engagement in indie game success. The project was a one-person endeavor that brought together various skills and demonstrated a holistic approach to indie game development and marketing.
